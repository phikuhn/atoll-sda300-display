## Why This Exists

The **Atoll SDA-300** already has a really good display. The font is large so you can actually read it from your couch, the layout is clear, navigation is usable. This is not a broken experience and that deserves to be said upfront.

The hardware surrounding this screen is in a category of its own. Hand-assembled in France, acoustically extraordinary. What comes out of this stereo amplifier is music. A sound you can almost touch. At this level, every detail matters, including the display you look at every time you interact with it.

This proposal is not a reinvention. It is a single degree of refinement: just enough modernity to make the display feel like it was designed by the same hands that designed the amplifier itself.

---

## Design Principles

**1. Don't fix what isn't broken.** Screen is good, navigation logic is fine. This preserves what works and refines what could be better.

**2. One degree of modernization.** Slightly more contemporary and refined visual hierarchy, improved contrast, nothing that changes the character of the interface.

**3. Match the app, match the hardware.** This display redesign is a direct companion to the [Atoll Signature app redesign I also created. Both surfaces now speak the same visual language.

**4. Atoll's DNA only.** Unmistakably Atoll: warm, precise, direct and deeply French.

---

## What Was Redesigned

- **Typography** — tighter spacing, clearer weight distribution between primary and secondary information
- **Visual hierarchy** — source, track, volume dominate; format and metadata recede gracefully
- **Layout consistency** — unified grid across all screen states
- **App coherence** — key visual elements aligned with the Atoll Signature mobile app redesign

---

## How It Was Built

Designed using **Claude Design** as a UX and design assistant. Part of a broader personal exploration of AI-assisted design applied to real products with genuine room for improvement.
